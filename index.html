Our Story

About Lafia:

How it all began:

In Novenber 2023, we chose a captone project. And as Artificial Inteligence students we had to seperat ourselves from our classmates, so we chose to do what was never done before in Niger. To create a medical chatbot. And now here we are.

Lafia is a Chatbot capable of giving accurate medical advise, and is capable of conecting with users emotionally.

How can you support us?

If you believe in our vision to make Niger more accessible to healthcare through technology, please do not hesitate to contact us through LinkedIn, Email or our social media platforms.

Why Lafia:

Imagine this scenario: You reside in a remote region of your country, where the once-paved road has succumbed to the relentless sun, leaving behind a treacherous path of mud during the rainy season. You toil on your land, your children trek to the nearest school by foot, and the absence of a vehicle hampers your mobility. Each week, you embark on a lengthy journey to the closest village to trade and market your harvest, consuming precious time. Seeking basic medical assistance at a health post proves unreliable, often met with closed doors due to insufficient medical supplies. In times of urgent medical need, the city beckons, yet the distance is insurmountable by foot, hours away from your village. This narrative encapsulates the challenges encountered in Niger, a vast sub-Saharan nation 40 times the size of Belgium. Despite the Nigerien government's endeavors, the quest for accessible and quality healthcare remains a formidable obstacle.

That is why we introduced Lafia. Lafia is dedicated to enhancing healthcare accessibility for Nigeriens. Beyond being a product of a capstone project, Lafia embodies a deeper purpose. It is about transforming lives and making a tangible difference. As Nigeriens who have experienced the challenges of the healthcare system firsthand, we are committed to improving the experience for others. Lafia is our way of addressing the hardships we have faced and striving to create a better future for healthcare in Niger.
